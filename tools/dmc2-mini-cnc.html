What You Need to Learn

  • Secure small stock safely.
  • Choose an approved bit/tool.
  • Set zero accurately.
  • Preview the toolpath before cutting.
  • Stop immediately for chatter or unexpected motion.

Certification Focus

This page prepares you for the hands-on check. Passing the quiz does not replace the instructor sign-off.

Ask before acting: If the setup does not match the training, stop and ask your instructor.
Real FabLab Scenario: The CNC begins to chatter during the first pass. What should happen? Stop/pause and check workholding, feeds/speeds, tool condition, and depth with the instructor.

Student Procedure

  1. Confirm material, stock size, and cutting tool.
  2. Secure stock with the approved workholding method.
  3. Load the approved toolpath and check the simulation.
  4. Set work zero carefully.
  5. Confirm clearance above clamps and fixtures.
  6. Begin the first pass under instructor supervision.
  7. Stop for chatter, bit deflection, burning, or loose stock.

Before You Start

  • Stock cannot move.
  • Toolpath avoids clamps.
  • Correct bit installed.
  • Emergency stop is known.

Stop Immediately If...

  • The tool makes an unusual sound.
  • The material moves unexpectedly.
  • You smell burning, see smoke, or notice heat where it should not be.
  • You are unsure what the next step should be.

Failure-Based Learning: If This Happens → Do This

What You NoticeBest Response
ChatterStop; check workholding, feed, RPM, tool stickout, and depth of cut.
BurningStop; reduce rubbing, check chip load, tool sharpness, and feed/speed.
Stock movesEmergency stop; never hold moving stock by hand.
Wrong depthStop; re-check zero and toolpath setup.
Bit deflectionReduce cutting load and verify correct tool.
