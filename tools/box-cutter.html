Box Cutter Training

Use a box cutter for cardboard, foam board, and light sheet materials while controlling blade exposure and cutting direction.

What You Need to Learn

  • Use minimal blade exposure.
  • Cut away from your body.
  • Use a cutting mat or approved surface.
  • Retract the blade immediately after use.

Certification Focus

This page prepares you for the hands-on check. Passing the quiz does not replace the instructor sign-off.

Ask before acting: If the setup does not match the training, stop and ask your instructor.
Real FabLab Scenario: A student cuts toward their other hand. What should happen? Stop and reposition the material and hand before continuing.

Student Procedure

  1. Confirm a box cutter is the correct tool for the material.
  2. Place material on an approved cutting surface.
  3. Expose only the blade length needed.
  4. Keep your other hand out of the cutting path.
  5. Make controlled passes instead of forcing one deep cut.
  6. Retract the blade and return the tool.

Before You Start

  • Blade minimal.
  • Cut path clear.
  • Approved surface used.
  • Blade retracted.

Stop Immediately If...

  • The tool makes an unusual sound.
  • The material moves unexpectedly.
  • You smell burning, see smoke, or notice heat where it should not be.
  • You are unsure what the next step should be.
