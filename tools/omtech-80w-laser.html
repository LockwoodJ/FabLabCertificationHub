What You Need to Learn

  • Confirm materials are laser safe and approved.
  • Separate cut, score, and engrave operations.
  • Focus the laser and confirm ventilation/air assist.
  • Run test settings before final jobs when needed.
  • Monitor the full job and stop for unsafe conditions.

Certification Focus

This page prepares you for the hands-on check. Passing the quiz does not replace the instructor sign-off.

Ask before acting: If the setup does not match the training, stop and ask your instructor.
Real FabLab Scenario: A student starts an OMTech laser job and walks away. What should happen? Stop the unsafe behavior immediately and notify the instructor.

Student Procedure

  1. Confirm the material is approved for the laser.
  2. Prepare artwork with clear cut, score, and engrave layers/colors.
  3. Place material flat on the bed and check for scraps or obstructions.
  4. Turn on required ventilation and air assist.
  5. Focus using the classroom-approved method.
  6. Run a test grid or small sample if settings are uncertain.
  7. Monitor the full job and stop for flame, heavy smoke, or unexpected behavior.

Before You Start

  • Ventilation is on.
  • Material is approved.
  • Focus is set.
  • Student stays at machine.

Stop Immediately If...

  • The tool makes an unusual sound.
  • The material moves unexpectedly.
  • You smell burning, see smoke, or notice heat where it should not be.
  • You are unsure what the next step should be.

Failure-Based Learning: If This Happens → Do This

What You NoticeBest Response
Heavy smokeStop and check ventilation, material approval, and settings.
Small flameStop or pause according to instructor procedure; never leave the machine.
Weak cutCheck focus, speed/power, material thickness, and lens cleanliness with instructor.
Excessive burningIncrease speed, reduce power, improve focus, or test settings.
Unknown smell/materialStop immediately; do not cut unknown plastics.
