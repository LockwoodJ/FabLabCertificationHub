What You Need to Learn

  • Keep fingers away from hot glue and nozzle.
  • Use small amounts of glue.
  • Let joints cool before testing strength.
  • Keep the station clean.

Certification Focus

This page prepares you for the hands-on check. Passing the quiz does not replace the instructor sign-off.

Ask before acting: If the setup does not match the training, stop and ask your instructor.
Real FabLab Scenario: Hot glue gets on skin. What should happen? Stop and follow classroom first-aid procedure; notify the instructor.

Student Procedure

  1. Protect the work surface.
  2. Allow the glue gun to heat in a safe resting position.
  3. Apply only the amount of glue needed.
  4. Press parts together without touching molten glue.
  5. Let the joint cool before handling.
  6. Unplug and store according to classroom procedure.

Before You Start

  • Stand/safe rest used.
  • Nozzle not touched.
  • Glue controlled.
  • Station cleaned.

Stop Immediately If...

  • The tool makes an unusual sound.
  • The material moves unexpectedly.
  • You smell burning, see smoke, or notice heat where it should not be.
  • You are unsure what the next step should be.
