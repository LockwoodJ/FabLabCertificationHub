DeWalt Heat Gun Training

Use the heat gun for controlled heating tasks while protecting skin, surfaces, and nearby materials.

What You Need to Learn

  • Keep the nozzle away from skin and flammable materials.
  • Use heat-resistant surfaces.
  • Allow cooldown before storage.
  • Avoid overheating plastics.

Certification Focus

This page prepares you for the hands-on check. Passing the quiz does not replace the instructor sign-off.

Ask before acting: If the setup does not match the training, stop and ask your instructor.
Real FabLab Scenario: Plastic begins bubbling and smoking. What should happen? Stop, increase distance/lower heat, and notify the instructor if needed.

Student Procedure

  1. Confirm the task is approved for heat-gun use.
  2. Clear the area of flammable scraps.
  3. Choose a safe heat setting and keep the nozzle moving.
  4. Use pliers, clamps, or fixtures rather than holding hot material by hand.
  5. Set the heat gun on a safe surface while cooling.
  6. Return only after it is safe to store.

Before You Start

  • Area clear.
  • Surface protected.
  • Nozzle pointed safely.
  • Tool cooled before storage.

Stop Immediately If...

  • The tool makes an unusual sound.
  • The material moves unexpectedly.
  • You smell burning, see smoke, or notice heat where it should not be.
  • You are unsure what the next step should be.
