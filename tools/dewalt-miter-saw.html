DeWalt Miter Saw Training

The miter saw is used for accurate crosscuts and angled cuts. It is a high-risk tool and must be instructor supervised.

What You Need to Learn

  • Identify the no-hand zone.
  • Secure long material support.
  • Wait for full blade speed before cutting.
  • Wait for full blade stop before raising/removing material.

Certification Focus

This page prepares you for the hands-on check. Passing the quiz does not replace the instructor sign-off.

Ask before acting: If the setup does not match the training, stop and ask your instructor.
Real FabLab Scenario: A cut finishes but the blade is still spinning. What should happen? Keep the saw down and wait for full stop before removing material.

Student Procedure

  1. Get instructor approval for the cut.
  2. Mark the cut line and check material support.
  3. Keep hands outside the no-hand zone.
  4. Bring the blade to full speed before entering the material.
  5. Cut with steady control, then keep the blade down until it stops.
  6. Remove offcuts only after the blade has fully stopped.

Before You Start

  • Instructor present.
  • Hands outside danger zone.
  • Material supported.
  • Blade stopped before removing material.

Stop Immediately If...

  • The tool makes an unusual sound.
  • The material moves unexpectedly.
  • You smell burning, see smoke, or notice heat where it should not be.
  • You are unsure what the next step should be.
